# Awareness Angle Mapper

**Category:** messaging
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose

This Skill maps creative angles to the five stages of audience awareness — Unaware, Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware, and Most Aware — and ensures each piece of creative is speaking the right language to the right audience at the right stage of their journey. Most ad creative fails because it speaks to one awareness stage while running to audiences at a completely different stage. This Skill closes that gap.

---

## When to Use

- You're creating creative for a new audience and need to identify what awareness stage they're at
- Your ads have high CTR but terrible CVR — a classic awareness mismatch symptom
- You're building a full-funnel creative strategy and need to map what creative goes where
- You have a top-of-funnel and bottom-of-funnel audience and need different angles for each
- You're briefing a creative team and want to give them clear angle direction by audience tier
- You want to audit existing creative and identify whether it's matched to the right awareness stage

---

## Inputs Required

- Description of the product or offer
- Description of the target audience (demographics, psychographics, where they're coming from)
- The campaign or funnel stage this creative will run in (cold traffic, warm traffic, retargeting, etc.)
- Optional: existing ad copy or concepts to audit for awareness mismatch
- Optional: customer reviews, testimonials, or research describing how customers talk about the problem/solution

---

## What This Skill Does

Identifies the awareness stage of the target audience, maps appropriate angle categories to that stage, and generates specific creative angle directions (not full copy) for each stage. Also audits existing creative for awareness mismatch and recommends corrections.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Define the five awareness stages for this product**
   Translate the generic stages into product-specific language:
   - **Unaware:** Has the problem but doesn't know it's a problem or doesn't have a name for it. Has never searched for a solution.
   - **Problem Aware:** Knows they have the problem. Is frustrated by it. Has not yet discovered solutions or looked for them actively.
   - **Solution Aware:** Knows solutions exist. Researching options. Has not evaluated your product specifically.
   - **Product Aware:** Knows your product exists. Has visited your site, seen your ads, or heard your name. Has not purchased.
   - **Most Aware:** Knows your product, knows the price, has considered buying, just needs the right offer or reason to act.

2. **Classify the target audience by stage**
   Based on the audience description and traffic source, classify: What awareness stage is the majority of this audience at? Cold traffic is almost always Unaware or Problem Aware. Warm traffic (site visitors, video viewers, page engagers) is Solution Aware to Product Aware. Retargeting is Product Aware to Most Aware.

3. **Map appropriate angle types to each stage**
   For each stage, define the type of message that is appropriate:
   - **Unaware:** Education-first. Identify the hidden problem. Pattern interrupt to create recognition. Don't mention your product first.
   - **Problem Aware:** Agitation + empathy. Validate the pain, amplify the cost of inaction. Position solution without selling yet.
   - **Solution Aware:** Differentiation. Why your solution is better than alternatives. What makes your approach unique.
   - **Product Aware:** Proof + objection handling. Testimonials, case studies, specific results, risk reversal. Trust builders.
   - **Most Aware:** Offer + urgency. Make it easy to buy. Reduce friction. Specific deal, deadline, or bonus.

4. **Generate angle directions for the target stage**
   Produce 5-8 specific angle directions (not full copy) appropriate to the identified awareness stage. Each angle direction must include: the entry point of the message, the emotional job it does, and a one-line description of what the hook would communicate.

5. **Audit existing creative for awareness mismatch** (if provided)
   For each existing ad, identify what awareness stage it's written for and compare to the target audience stage. Flag mismatches with specific explanation.

6. **Build the angle map**
   If creative is running across multiple audience stages (full-funnel), produce a complete angle map: which angles go to which audience tiers, in what sequence, with what transition logic between stages.

## Output Requirements

**Audience Awareness Classification**
- Target audience stage (with rationale)
- What this audience knows, believes, and feels at this stage
- What they need to hear before they'll take action

**Angle Directions by Stage**
For the target stage: 5-8 specific angle directions, each with: entry point, emotional job, hook direction.

**Full Angle Map** (for full-funnel requests)

---

## Best Practices Applied

- Awareness stage determines message, not the other way around — you don't decide what to say and then find an audience
- Most cold traffic creative fails because it speaks at Product Aware level to Unaware audiences
- Retargeting creative fails when it still educates instead of closing
- Angle directions are entry points — they dictate where the conversation starts, which determines whether the audience engages
- Full-funnel creative strategy requires explicit transition logic between stages

---

## Guardrails

- Do not assume cold traffic is product-aware — this is the most expensive assumption in paid social
- Do not write angle directions that require prior knowledge the audience doesn't have
- Do not map the same angle to multiple awareness stages without explanation
- Do not confuse audience segment with awareness stage — the same demographic can be at different stages
- Do not apply "most aware" urgency copy to unaware audiences — it will feel irrelevant and confusing
