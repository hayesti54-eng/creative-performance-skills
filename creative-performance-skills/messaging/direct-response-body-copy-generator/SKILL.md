# Direct Response Body Copy Generator

**Category:** messaging
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose

This Skill writes complete, conversion-oriented ad body copy using the foundational direct-response sequence: Hook → Agitate → Prove → CTA. It does not write brand copy. It does not write "engaging" copy. It writes copy engineered to move a specific audience from indifference or resistance to action — using the principles that have driven direct-response advertising for decades, adapted for the context of modern paid social.

---

## When to Use

- You need complete body copy for a new ad creative
- Your existing copy is not converting and you need a structured rewrite
- You want to test multiple copy angles against the same visual
- You're briefing a copywriter and want to show them the structure and standard to hit
- You want to generate 5-10 body copy variations for A/B testing from a single angle direction
- You need copy that works as both long-form and short-form (you'll get both)

---

## Inputs Required

- Product or service description (features + benefits + outcomes)
- Target audience (who specifically, what they know, what they feel, what they want)
- Primary angle or hook direction (what emotional/strategic angle this copy will use)
- Awareness stage of audience
- Offer details (price, guarantee, what's included, deadline if applicable)
- Key proof elements (testimonials, stats, results, social proof)
- CTA (what should the audience do, what do they get when they click)
- Tone guide (optional — conversational, authoritative, empathetic, urgent, etc.)
- Character / word limit (optional — specify if platform constraints apply)

---

## What This Skill Does

Generates complete ad body copy in the Hook → Agitate → Prove → CTA structure, in both short-form (under 150 words) and long-form (250-400 words) versions, with 3 full variations using different emotional angles.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Establish the copy foundation**
   Before writing, document: (a) the one primary emotion being activated in this copy, (b) the one outcome being promised, (c) the one proof element being used, (d) the one action being requested. Every element of the copy must serve one of these four purposes — if it doesn't, it's cut.

2. **Write the Hook (Lines 1-2)**
   The hook must stop the scroll within the first 2 lines. Rules:
   - Specific over general: "Lost 22 lbs in 47 days" beats "Lost weight fast"
   - Problem or outcome, not product
   - Must speak to the awareness stage (unaware = problem recognition; product aware = proof or offer)
   - Pattern interrupt optional but not required — specificity alone interrupts
   - Under 20 words

3. **Write the Agitate layer (Lines 3-6)**
   Deepen the problem or amplify the desired outcome. Make the cost of inaction real. Options:
   - List 3 specific frustrations the audience experiences right now
   - Describe the gap between where they are and where they want to be
   - Ask a question that makes them confront the problem directly
   - Introduce the villain (what's been preventing the solution)

4. **Write the Prove layer (Lines 7-12)**
   Deliver proof that the solution works. Proof must be specific, not aspirational. Options:
   - Specific customer result with timeframe: "[Name] went from X to Y in Z weeks"
   - Social proof with number: "Over 14,000 customers have used [product] since 2021"
   - Process proof: a brief "how it works" that makes the mechanism credible
   - Authority: credentials, certifications, media mentions with specific outlet

5. **Write the CTA layer (Lines 13-15)**
   The CTA must do three things: name the action, describe what happens next, and reduce the final friction point. "Click Learn More to see the exact protocol [customer name] used — and try it risk-free for 30 days."

6. **Write short-form version**
   Compress to under 150 words by keeping only the highest-impact sentence from each layer. The structure remains, the volume contracts.

7. **Write 3 angle variations**
   Variation A: The "outcome-first" version — leads with the transformation
   Variation B: The "problem-first" version — leads with the pain
   Variation C: The "proof-first" version — leads with the result or testimonial

---

## Output Requirements

**Copy Foundation Statement**
One sentence: "This copy activates [emotion], promises [outcome], proves it with [proof], and asks for [CTA]."

**3 Full Long-Form Variations** (250-400 words each)
Each labeled with angle type, emotional register, and awareness stage match.

**3 Short-Form Versions** (under 150 words each)
One per long-form variation, compressed.

**Headline Pairings**
3-5 headline options for each variation (headline = ad post title or opening card, distinct from first body copy line).

**Copy Mechanics Notes**
For each variation: a brief note on what specific DR move is doing the heavy lifting and why.

---

## Best Practices Applied

- Every sentence must either hook, agitate, prove, or close — no decorative sentences
- Agitation is not negativity — it is activating the emotional reality the audience already lives in
- Proof must be specific — vague social proof is worse than no proof because it signals inauthenticity
- CTAs must describe what happens when they click — "Learn More" without context is weak
- Short-form is not a summary — it's a compressed version with the same structure

---

## Guardrails

- Do not write brand copy disguised as direct response — "We believe in X" has no place in DR body copy
- Do not use passive voice in hooks or CTAs — active language only
- Do not introduce more than one primary promise — multiple promises dilute conversion
- Do not pad word count with connector phrases that carry no meaning
- Do not write CTAs that increase anxiety ("Act now before it's too late!") for low-urgency offers — authenticity matters
