# Voice of Customer Hook Builder

**Category:** messaging
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose

This Skill mines real customer language — from reviews, testimonials, sales calls, Reddit threads, comment sections, and survey responses — and converts it into high-performing ad hooks. Generic hooks fail because they sound like a marketer wrote them. Hooks built from real customer language convert because the audience reads them and thinks "this was written for me." This Skill is the bridge between raw customer voice and deployed performance creative.

---

## When to Use

- You have a pile of customer reviews or testimonials and want to extract hooks from them
- Your current hooks feel generic and aren't stopping the scroll
- You want to run a new creative round and want every hook grounded in real language
- You've collected survey responses or sales call notes and want to turn them into creative
- You want to test emotional angle variations using authentic customer expressions
- You're entering a new market and want to understand what language the audience uses

---

## Inputs Required

- Raw customer language (paste in the actual text): reviews, testimonials, survey responses, sales call transcripts, Reddit/forum posts, comment section excerpts, objection logs
- Product or service description (2-5 sentences)
- Awareness stage being targeted
- Primary emotion to tap: frustration, aspiration, fear, desire, skepticism, curiosity
- The one outcome or transformation the product delivers
- Optional: existing hooks that have performed well or poorly

---

## What This Skill Does

Analyzes raw customer language for the most repeated, emotionally charged phrases and converts them into structured ad hooks using five hook formulas. Outputs 10-20 ready-to-test hooks organized by emotional register and awareness stage.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Mine for signal phrases**
   Read through all provided customer language and extract: phrases that repeat across multiple customers, phrases that carry strong emotional charge (frustration, relief, transformation, fear), specific words customers use to describe the problem, specific words customers use to describe the outcome, and phrases that feel surprising, raw, or unexpectedly specific.

2. **Categorize the language by emotional register**
   Sort extracted phrases into: (a) Pain language (frustration, disappointment, suffering), (b) Aspiration language (desire, hope, outcome), (c) Skepticism language (objections, "I thought," "I didn't believe"), (d) Relief language (after the transformation, looking back), (e) Identity language (how they see themselves, who they are/were).

3. **Identify the most powerful phrase in each category**
   The power of a phrase is measured by: specificity (the more specific, the more it resonates), emotional charge (does it activate a strong feeling?), and universality within the target audience (would many people in this audience recognize themselves in this phrase?).

4. **Apply the five hook formulas**
   For each high-power phrase, generate hooks using these structures:
   - **The Quote Drop:** Use the exact customer phrase as the hook. "I tried everything for 3 years and nothing worked." — [Name], [Result after using product]
   - **The Outcome Statement:** Convert the transformation into a specific, numbered claim. "Lost 18 lbs in 6 weeks without counting a single calorie."
   - **The Problem Agitator:** Lead with the pain, amplified. "Still [doing X painful thing] every day and wondering why [desired outcome] isn't happening?"
   - **The Pattern Interrupt:** Say the thing they're thinking that no one says in ads. "Every productivity app told me to wake up at 5am. I tried 11 of them. None of them worked for me."
   - **The Identity Hook:** Call out who they are. "For freelancers who charge by the hour and keep losing track of where the time went."

5. **Write 10-20 specific hooks**
   Apply the formulas across the extracted phrases. Each hook must: use specific language from the research (not generic replacements), be under 25 words, be addressable to the awareness stage specified, activate one clear emotion.

6. **Add a "direct lift" option for each hook**
   Alongside each crafted hook, provide a "direct lift" version that quotes the customer as literally as possible. Direct lifts often outperform crafted hooks because they are 100% authentic.

7. **Flag the top 3 hooks for immediate testing**
   Based on specificity, emotional charge, and awareness stage fit, flag the three hooks most likely to stop the scroll for this audience.

---

## Output Requirements

**Signal Phrase Library**
Top 10-15 extracted phrases organized by emotional category.

**Hook Library**
20 hooks minimum, organized by formula type and emotional register. For each:
- Hook text
- Formula used
- Emotional register
- Awareness stage targeted
- Direct lift version (where applicable)

**Top 3 Immediate Test Recommendations**
With brief rationale for each: why this specific hook is likely to perform for this audience.

**Language Patterns Noted**
Any recurring language patterns across the customer base that should inform body copy and offer framing beyond just hooks.

---

## Best Practices Applied

- The best hooks are found, not written — mining customer language always outperforms invention
- Specificity in hooks signals relevance — "lost 18 lbs" converts better than "lost weight"
- Direct lifts should always be tested alongside crafted variations
- Hooks must match the awareness stage — "if you've tried X and it didn't work" only resonates with people who have tried X
- The emotional register of the hook must match the emotional state of the audience at entry

---

## Guardrails

- Do not invent language that isn't in the customer research — only use what real customers have said
- Do not use generic marketing phrasing ("transform your life," "achieve your goals") — these are the enemy
- Do not write hooks that require the audience to know your product exists (for cold traffic / unaware stages)
- Do not use aspirational hooks for Problem Aware audiences who need validation before aspiration
- Do not make specific outcome claims unless a customer has actually said them — accuracy is required
