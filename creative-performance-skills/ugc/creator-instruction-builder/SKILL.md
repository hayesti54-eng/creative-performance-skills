# Creator Instruction Builder

**Category:** ugc
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose

This Skill generates the creator-facing instruction packet that accompanies a UGC brief — the practical, operational document that tells a creator exactly what to submit, how to submit it, what formats are required, what will get their submission rejected, and what a winning submission looks like. Without creator instructions, even a perfect brief produces inconsistent, unusable content. This Skill ensures the handoff from brand to creator is professional and frictionless.

---

## When to Use

- You're sending briefs to UGC creators on any platform and want to reduce back-and-forth
- You're building a creator onboarding packet for your brand's UGC program
- A creator has submitted unusable content and you need to brief the reshoot clearly
- You're managing multiple creators and need a standardized submission process
- You want to set quality and content standards upfront so there are no disputes at review

---

## Inputs Required

- Platform(s) the content will be published on (Meta Feed, Reels, TikTok, Stories, YouTube Shorts)
- Video length required (exact seconds)
- Number of deliverables expected from this brief (main video + hooks? b-roll? photos?)
- File format requirements (MP4, MOV, resolution, aspect ratio)
- Deadline for first submission
- Review process (how many rounds of revisions are included)
- Brand name, any required on-screen text overlays, logo usage rules
- Hard restrictions (what the creator must never do or say)
- Payment terms and revision policy (optional)

---

## What This Skill Does

Generates a complete, professionally formatted creator instruction document that covers: submission checklist, technical requirements, content standards, rejection criteria, revision process, and reference examples.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Define the deliverables list**
   List every file the creator must submit: main video, hook variations, raw footage (if required), thumbnail, b-roll clips, still photos. Be explicit about quantity: "We need 1 main video + 3 hook variations + 4 b-roll clips."

2. **Write the technical specifications**
   Resolution: minimum 1080p, ideally 4K for feeds. Aspect ratio: 9:16 for Reels/TikTok/Stories, 1:1 or 4:5 for feed. Format: MP4 preferred, MOV acceptable. Audio: clean, no background music from creator (brand adds music in post). File naming convention.

3. **Write the content standards**
   What good looks like: natural delivery, on-message, correct proof points hit, CTA included. What bad looks like (common failures): reading off a script, looking at the phone camera incorrectly, unnatural pauses, incorrect product name pronunciation, background noise.

4. **Write the rejection criteria**
   Be direct about what will result in a requested reshoot: (a) wrong product name or claim, (b) required CTA missing, (c) audio quality unusable, (d) required proof point not included, (e) obvious script-reading, (f) resolution below minimum.

5. **Write the revision policy**
   Number of revisions included. What constitutes a revision request vs. a full reshoot. Turnaround time required. Process: how revisions are communicated (written notes vs. timestamped feedback).

6. **Write the submission process**
   Where to submit (Google Drive link, WeTransfer, platform upload). File naming convention. Deadline. What confirmation looks like (creator receives acknowledgment within X hours/days).

7. **Write the reference quality examples**
   Describe (or link) 1-2 examples of content that meets the brand's standard. If no prior examples exist, describe the standard in plain terms: "Your submission should feel like a genuine recommendation from a trusted friend, not a commercial. Think Instagram Stories reel, not TV commercial."

8. **Write the "Before You Submit" checklist**
   A self-review checklist the creator goes through before hitting send:
   - [ ] Video is the correct length (±3 seconds of target)
   - [ ] Opening hook is one of the provided options (or natural ad-lib that hits same message)
   - [ ] Product name said correctly and at least once clearly
   - [ ] CTA included and audible
   - [ ] Correct aspect ratio
   - [ ] No background music recorded
   - [ ] Proof point mentioned naturally
   - [ ] Face visible (if required)

## Output Requirements

**Creator Instruction Document**
Formatted as a professional brief document the creator can read in under 5 minutes:

```
CREATOR INSTRUCTIONS: [Brand Name] x [Brief Title]

DELIVERABLES REQUIRED
[Full list with quantity and file specs]

TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
[Resolution, aspect ratio, format, audio, naming]

CONTENT STANDARDS
[What good looks like — plain English]

REJECTION CRITERIA
[What will require a reshoot — specific and non-negotiable]

REVISION POLICY
[Number of rounds, turnaround, process]

HOW TO SUBMIT
[Where, when, what confirmation looks like]

BEFORE YOU SUBMIT CHECKLIST
[ ] Item 1
[ ] Item 2
...

QUESTIONS?
[Contact method]
```

---

## Best Practices Applied

- Rejection criteria must be written before submission — not discovered during review
- The "Before You Submit" checklist reduces the need for revision rounds by catching common errors at source
- Revision policy must be explicit — ambiguity leads to disputes
- Technical specs are non-negotiable — content that doesn't meet them cannot be used regardless of creative quality
- Plain language throughout — creators are not marketers

---

## Guardrails

- Do not write instructions that are longer than the creator will actually read — cut ruthlessly
- Do not bury rejection criteria at the end — they must be visible before submission
- Do not use marketing language in creator instructions — operational language only
- Do not assume the creator knows the product — always include product pronunciation guide if non-obvious
- Do not leave submission logistics ambiguous — "send it our way when ready" is not a submission process
