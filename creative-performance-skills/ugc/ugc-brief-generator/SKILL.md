# UGC Brief Generator

**Category:** ugc
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose

This Skill generates complete, production-ready UGC briefs for creators — whether professional UGC creators, micro-influencers, customers, or internal team members. A bad UGC brief produces generic, unusable content. A good UGC brief gives the creator everything they need to record a natural, authentic-feeling ad that hits specific direct-response objectives. This Skill bridges the gap between marketing strategy and creator execution.

---

## When to Use

- You're commissioning UGC content from a paid creator or platform (Billo, Fiverr, Insense, etc.)
- You're asking a customer or brand advocate to film a video
- You have an internal team member creating UGC-style content
- You want multiple UGC variations from the same creator with different hooks or angles
- You're building a UGC library for testing across multiple creative formats
- You want to brief an AI video tool that requires scene-by-scene instructions

---

## Inputs Required

- Product or service description (what it is, what it does, what problem it solves)
- Target audience and their primary pain point
- Primary angle for this brief (e.g., "problem-solution," "before-after," "testimonial," "demo")
- Key proof points or claims to include (results, ingredients, certifications, outcomes)
- Offer to mention (optional — if there's a promo, discount, or CTA to include)
- Tone: authentic/conversational, high-energy, calm and credible, educational, etc.
- Video length target (15s, 30s, 60s)
- Platform (Meta Feed, Reels/TikTok, Stories)
- Any hard restrictions (no competitor mentions, required disclaimers, brand name pronunciation)

---

## What This Skill Does

Generates a complete, creator-ready UGC brief with: concept overview, hook script options, scene-by-scene breakdown, delivery style notes, proof points to hit, CTA, filming instructions, and variation notes for retakes.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Define the creative objective**
   What is this UGC supposed to do? Drive CTR (hook-focused), drive CVR (proof-focused), reduce objections (objection-handling), build trust (testimonial), or demonstrate the product (demo)? The objective determines every brief decision.

2. **Write the concept overview**
   One paragraph for the creator explaining: what the brand does, who this is for, and what the creator is playing in this ad (authentic user, expert, before-after subject, demonstrator, etc.). Write this in plain English, not marketing language.

3. **Write 2-3 hook options**
   Give the creator 2-3 hook scripts for the opening 3-5 seconds. Each hook should be different in style (question, statement, action-first) so the creator can choose the one that feels most natural. Include delivery direction: "Say this like you're telling a friend, not reading from a script."

4. **Write scene-by-scene breakdown**
   Break the video into scenes with:
   - Scene number and timestamp (approx.)
   - What to do or say
   - Camera framing note (close-up, medium, product in-hand, etc.)
   - Delivery note (pace, energy, tone for this specific scene)
   - B-roll suggestion (if applicable)

5. **Write proof points to hit**
   List 2-4 specific facts, results, or claims the creator must work in naturally. For each, provide: the claim, an example of how to say it naturally, and an instruction on delivery ("Don't read this off a card — work it into the sentence before the CTA").

6. **Write the CTA instruction**
   Exact words to use. Delivery style. What to do physically (hold up phone, look directly at camera, hold product). Where to place it in the video (typically 5-10 seconds before end, not at the very end).

7. **Write filming instructions**
   Location, lighting, wardrobe, device (phone recommended for authenticity unless studio content), audio, whether to show face, b-roll requirements, retake guidance.

8. **Write variation notes**
   If the client wants multiple deliverables from one shoot, specify the 2-3 variations: "Record one version with the price hook and one with the results hook. Everything else stays the same."

## Output Requirements

**Complete UGC Brief Document**

```
BRIEF TITLE: [Format + Angle + Platform]
CONCEPT OVERVIEW: [Plain English paragraph for creator]
OBJECTIVE: [Drive CTR / CVR / Trust / Demo]
VIDEO LENGTH: [target]
PLATFORM: [Meta Feed / Reels / Stories]

HOOK OPTIONS: (choose one, feel free to ad-lib slightly)
  Option A: [exact script + delivery note]
  Option B: [exact script + delivery note]
  Option C: [exact script + delivery note]

SCENE BREAKDOWN:
  Scene 1 (0:00-0:05): [action + script + framing + delivery note]
  Scene 2 (0:05-0:20): [action + script + framing + delivery note]
  Scene 3 (0:20-0:35): [action + script + framing + delivery note]
  Scene 4 (0:35-0:45): [CTA + action + delivery note]

PROOF POINTS TO HIT:
  - [Claim 1 + natural delivery example]
  - [Claim 2 + natural delivery example]

CTA: [Exact words + delivery + timing]

FILMING INSTRUCTIONS:
  - Location: [specific]
  - Lighting: [specific]
  - Device: [phone / camera]
  - Wardrobe: [specific or "casual, clean, on-brand color if possible"]
  - Audio: [natural / quiet room / no background music needed]
  - Face: [yes / optional]

RETAKE GUIDANCE: [Common mistakes to avoid, what a good take looks like]

VARIATIONS NEEDED:
  - Version A: [description]
  - Version B: [description]
```

---

## Best Practices Applied

- Hook options give creators agency, which produces more natural delivery — never script a single hook and demand it verbatim
- Scene breakdowns prevent aimless rambling — creators stay on message when they know what each beat requires
- Proof points are embedded naturally, not recited as a list
- CTA placement (not last) prevents drop-off from killing the action instruction
- Filming instructions reduce post-production problems and reshoots

---

## Guardrails

- Do not over-script the delivery — UGC authenticity is destroyed by perfectly recited scripts
- Do not include more than 4 key messages — overloaded briefs produce confused, unfocused videos
- Do not assume the creator knows the brand — brief them from zero knowledge
- Do not skip filming instructions — poor production quality kills distribution quality
- Do not write CTAs that feel unnatural for the creator to say — test the CTA out loud before including it

## Example Requests

1. "I'm sending a brief to 3 UGC creators on Billo for our skincare product. Write me a complete 30-second UGC brief for a before-after angle targeting women 25-40 dealing with acne."

2. "Write a UGC demo brief for our kitchen gadget product. The creator should show the product working in real-time in a 45-second video with 3 hook options."

3. "I need 2 UGC variation briefs from the same creator — one leading with the problem (no time to cook), one leading with the outcome (meal prep done in 20 minutes). Give me full briefs for both."
