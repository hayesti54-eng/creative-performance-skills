# Testimonial Structure Planner

**Category:** ugc
**Tier:** pro

## Purpose

This Skill structures testimonials — whether written, video, or audio — into a direct-response format that maximizes conversion impact. A testimonial that says "I love this product!" is nearly worthless. A testimonial structured as: before state → specific change → specific outcome → unexpected benefit → endorsement → CTA is a conversion asset. This Skill transforms raw customer language into structured testimonial formats designed to do direct-response work.

---

## When to Use

- You have raw customer reviews or testimonials and want to structure them for ads
- You're filming customer testimonial videos and want a structured script framework
- You're building a testimonial library for retargeting creative
- You want to write prompted testimonial collection questions that produce better raw material
- You're advising a customer on what to say in their testimonial video
- You want to audit existing testimonials for DR-structure gaps and improve them

---

## Inputs Required

- Raw testimonial material (customer quote, review text, email, or video transcript)
- OR: a description of the customer result you want to capture (if writing testimonial prompts)
- Product or service being testimoniated
- The primary objection this testimonial should address (optional but powerful)
- The awareness stage of the audience who will see this testimonial
- Format (written quote for static ad, video script, audio)
- Platform and placement (feed, retargeting, website)

---

## What This Skill Does

Either structures existing raw testimonial content into a direct-response format, writes prompted questions to collect better testimonials, or produces a full structured testimonial script framework for video recording.

---

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Evaluate raw testimonial material (if provided)**
   Identify what's present and what's missing from the DR testimonial structure:
   - Before state (what was their situation before?)
   - Trigger (what made them try this?)
   - Specific outcome (what exact result did they get?)
   - Timeframe (how quickly?)
   - Unexpected benefit (what surprised them?)
   - Credibility signal (who are they? occupation, location, relevant context)
   - Endorsement (would they recommend it?)
   - Objection addressed (does it preemptively handle a known objection?)

2. **Score the raw material**
   How much DR structure is already present? If high: structure and present. If low: identify what questions would have produced the missing elements and note them for future collection.

3. **Structure the testimonial using the DR Testimonial Arc**
   **DR Testimonial Arc:**
   1. Credibility intro (who they are, why their opinion matters)
   2. Before state (the specific situation or struggle)
   3. Decision trigger (why they tried this specific product/solution)
   4. The specific result (with number and timeframe when possible)
   5. The unexpected benefit (what they didn't expect to also get)
   6. The objection they had before buying (and how it proved wrong)
   7. The recommendation (to someone "just like them")
   8. CTA bridge (natural lead into the call to action)

4. **Adapt for format**
   - Written quote: compress to the 2-3 most powerful sentences. Lead with outcome, not preamble.
   - Video script: full arc with camera direction notes for each beat
   - Pull quote for static ad: single sentence with specific result + name + brief identifier

5. **Write the testimonial collection questions** (if needed)
   Generate 8-12 questions designed to produce the missing DR elements naturally:
   - "Describe what was happening in your [life/business/health] before you found [product]."
   - "What specific result have you noticed, and how long did it take?"
   - "What were you most skeptical about before you tried it?"
   - "What would you say to someone who's thinking about trying it but isn't sure yet?"

6. **Write 3 format variations from the same testimonial**
   Short pull quote (under 30 words), medium written testimonial (75-100 words), full video script (using the DR arc).

---

## Output Requirements

**Testimonial Evaluation** (if raw material provided)
Score on DR elements present: ✓ present / ✗ missing / ~ partial. Elements missing = collection opportunity.

**Structured Testimonial Variations**
- Pull quote (under 30 words, lead with result)
- Medium written version (75-100 words)
- Video script using full DR arc with camera notes

**Testimonial Collection Questions**
8-12 questions to elicit each DR element from a customer. Formatted as a simple form or interview guide.

**Objection Bridge Note**
One sentence describing which objection this testimonial addresses and how to position it in ad targeting (e.g., "Run this testimonial in retargeting for abandoned carts — it addresses the price skepticism objection directly.").

---

## Best Practices Applied

- Specific outcomes convert better than emotional statements — "Lost 14 lbs" > "Felt so much better"
- Timeframes add credibility — "in 3 weeks" makes the claim verifiable and believable
- The unexpected benefit is often the highest-converting line — it adds dimensionality beyond the claimed outcome
- The objection addressed in the testimonial should match the dominant objection of the target audience
- The credibility intro must be relevant — an age, occupation, or situation the audience identifies with

---

## Guardrails

- Do not fabricate any testimonial element — structure and present only, never invent
- Do not strip out the customer's specific language in the pursuit of polish — real phrasing converts better than cleaned-up phrasing
- Do not lead a written testimonial with the customer's name — lead with the result or insight
- Do not use testimonials that make unverifiable medical, financial, or legal claims without appropriate disclaimers
- Do not use testimonials without appropriate usage rights confirmation
