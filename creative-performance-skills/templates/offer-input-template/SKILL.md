# Offer Input Template

**Category:** templates
**Tier:** free

## Purpose

This template structures the core offer so that every creative asset built around it is grounded in the economic engine of the campaign. The offer is the most important variable in performance creative — the creative's job is to make the offer irresistible to the right audience. Without a fully structured offer, creative teams invent vague "value propositions" instead of translating a real, compelling offer into compelling creative. This template prevents that.

---

## When to Use

- Before generating any ad copy or creative concepts
- When launching a new offer, promotion, or product version
- When restructuring an existing offer that isn't converting
- When the offer details aren't clearly documented anywhere
- Before running any landing page alignment audits (the offer must be clearly defined first)
- When briefing anyone — copywriter, designer, media buyer — on what they are selling

---

## Inputs Required

Complete this template directly. Pull details from internal documentation, product teams, or the founder.

## Analysis Workflow

1. Review for completeness — Section 2 (pricing), Section 3 (guarantee), and Section 7 (one-sentence offer) are required before generating any copy
2. Check urgency claims in Section 4 are real — do not allow false urgency in creative
3. Evaluate value stack in Section 2 — if total value is not meaningfully higher than price, the value stack angle will not work
4. Extract the one-sentence offer from Section 7 — this becomes the CTA foundation for all copy
5. Flag any guarantee gaps — a weak or missing guarantee is a conversion problem, not just a copy problem

---

## Output Requirements

**Offer Completeness Rating**
Flag any sections incomplete or vague that will limit copy quality.

**The Core Offer Statement**
The one-sentence offer from Section 7 — validated and polished.

**Top 3 Offer Angles**
The three most compelling ways to present this offer in creative: (1) outcome-first, (2) risk-reversal-first, (3) value-stack-first.

---

## Best Practices Applied

- The one-sentence offer in Section 7 is the most important output — every headline and CTA should be derivable from it
- Guarantee language must be accurate and specific — vague guarantees are less convincing than no guarantee
- Value stack only works if the components are genuinely valuable — invented "bonus" values destroy credibility
- Urgency must be real — false scarcity is audience-visible and trust-destroying

---

## Guardrails

- Do not create urgency or scarcity that does not exist — note explicitly when none is present
- Do not inflate bonus values to make the value stack look more impressive than it is
- Do not accept "great value" as an offer description — require specific components, prices, and outcomes
- Do not write CTAs before Section 7 (one-sentence offer) is complete
