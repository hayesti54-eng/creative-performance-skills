# Brand Brief Template

**Category:** templates
**Tier:** free

## Purpose

This template gathers all brand and product intelligence needed to run every other Skill in this system with maximum specificity. Fill this out once, and every creative output — hooks, copy, concepts, briefs, reports, audits — becomes instantly more relevant and accurate. Without a complete brand brief, every Skill runs on generic assumptions. With it, every Skill runs on real intelligence.

---

## When to Use

- Starting a new account or brand relationship
- Onboarding a new creative strategist or media buyer
- Before beginning any creative production round
- As the input document for the Meta Format Concept Engine, UGC Brief Generator, VOC Hook Builder, and every copy Skill in this system

---

## Inputs Required

Complete this template directly. No external inputs needed — it is the input.

---

## What This Skill Does

Structures a complete brand brief that captures: brand identity, product mechanism, ideal customer profile, pain points, objections, offer, proof, tone, constraints, and creative direction. Outputs a single reference document that feeds every other Skill.

## Analysis Workflow

1. Review the completed brief for gaps and flag any sections that are incomplete or vague
2. Identify the 3 most powerful proof elements from Section 7
3. Identify the dominant objection from Section 5 — this becomes the first test angle
4. Extract the audience's exact language from Section 3 (psychographics, how they describe the problem)
5. Flag any claims constraints from Section 9 that require careful copy review
6. Confirm the awareness stage priority from Section 13 before any creative is generated

---

## Output Requirements

**Brief Completeness Score**
Flag any section that is incomplete, vague, or that requires more specificity before generating creative.

**Top 3 Insights from the Brief**
The three most powerful inputs for creative generation (the strongest proof, the most potent pain point, the most relevant customer language).

**Recommended First Creative Direction**
Based on the completed brief: what is the strongest angle for the first round of creative? Which format category? Which awareness stage? Which objection to address first?

---

## Best Practices Applied

- Customer language in Section 3 is the highest-value section for hook generation
- Proof specificity in Section 7 determines how specific the ad copy can be
- Claims constraints in Section 9 prevent compliance problems from being discovered at the copy stage
- The brief is a living document — update it when new proof is available, new objections are discovered, or new creative learnings are made

---

## Guardrails

- Do not generate ad concepts until Section 3 (ICP), Section 5 (Objections), Section 6 (Offer), and Section 7 (Proof) are complete
- Do not accept "we have a great product" as a proof point — real results with real numbers are required
- Do not allow vague tone descriptions like "friendly but professional" — require specific examples
- Do not treat the brief as permanent — it must be updated as learnings accumulate
