# Creative Intake Template

**Category:** templates
**Tier:** free

## Purpose

This template captures everything needed to generate a specific round of ad concepts in one shot. Where the Brand Brief captures brand-level context that persists across campaigns, the Creative Intake captures campaign-specific context: the specific offer, audience, objective, format priorities, and creative direction for this particular production run. Fill this in before briefing any Skill in the concept-systems or messaging folders.

---

## When to Use

- Before generating any new creative concepts
- Before briefing a copywriter or creative team
- At the start of each new test round or campaign sprint
- When running a specific promotional campaign with unique offer details
- When changing the primary audience or creative angle from the previous round

---

## Inputs Required

Complete this template directly. Pull brand-level context from the Brand Brief Template. This template captures campaign-specific information only.

## Analysis Workflow

1. Review completed intake for gaps — flag any section missing required information before generating concepts
2. Cross-reference with Brand Brief to confirm no contradictions between intake and brief
3. Confirm the awareness stage in Section 3 — this determines which angles are appropriate
4. Verify the offer details in Section 2 are specific enough to write accurate copy (price, guarantee, deadline)
5. Check that the format priorities in Section 4 are matched to the audience and campaign objective

---

## Output Requirements

**Intake Completeness Check**
Flag any field left blank or vague that will limit creative quality. Priority flags: Section 2 (offer), Section 3 (audience awareness stage), Section 5 (proof to feature).

**Creative Brief Summary**
A single-paragraph synthesis of the intake, written as a creative direction statement: "For this round, we're targeting [audience] at [awareness stage] with a [angle]-led campaign featuring [proof type]. Priority formats are [1, 2, 3]. The goal is to move [metric] above [benchmark] by [date]."

---

## Best Practices Applied

- The intake captures campaign-specific context — it does not replace the brand brief, it supplements it
- Proof specificity in Section 5 directly determines copy specificity — vague proof produces vague copy
- Testing plan in Section 8 is filled out before concepts are generated — testing is planned, not decided later
- Deadline documentation in Section 9 prevents creative production bottlenecks

---

## Guardrails

- Do not proceed to concept generation without a completed Section 2 (offer) and Section 3 (audience)
- Do not accept "just make some ads" as the creative direction — require specific angle and format priorities
- Do not allow Section 8 testing plan to be skipped — if there's no test structure, note it explicitly
- Do not allow vague proof in Section 5 — require actual customer names, results, and timeframes
